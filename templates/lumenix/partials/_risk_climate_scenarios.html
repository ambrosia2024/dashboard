Climate-adjusted scenarios vs time

Data assumptions – climate-adjusted scenarios

Data assumptions

  • Data is simulated for the crop–pathogen pair currently selected (labels taken from local storage if present, otherwise Lettuce — Salmonella).
  • The period is fixed to monthly 2025-01 → 2035-12 to show a medium-term climate signal.
  • Each scenario applies a different warming slope:
    • Optimistic: low warming, mapped to RCP4.5.
    • Central: moderate warming, also RCP4.5.
    • Pessimistic: higher warming, mapped to RCP8.5.
  • Monthly temperature anomaly (Δ °C) is used as the main “climate driver”.
  • Probability of illness (%) is then nudged upwards over time in the same direction as the warming — this is to illustrate sensitivity, not to predict cases.
  • No real climate model output is read here; values are for demo/exploration only.

Read this as “if the area warmed like this, the modelled risk could move like this”, not as an actual forecast.

How this chart is built

  • date (month) is used on the X-axis.
  • temp_anomaly_c (Δ °C) is drawn as the main solid line on the left Y-axis.
  • prob_illness_pct (%) is drawn as a dashed line on the right Y-axis so the two scales don’t clash.
  • The scenario dropdown only changes the slope/size of these two lines.
  • Tooltips show the date, anomaly and probability for that month.
